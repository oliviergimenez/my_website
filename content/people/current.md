<img src="/img/camille.jpg" width="130"> **Camille Mottier** (PhD 2025-2029) is modelling the spatial dynamics of coypus at multiple scales. She is co-supervised by [Hoel Queffelec](https://hqueffel.pages.math.cnrs.fr/) and [Jessica Guérand](https://sites.google.com/view/guerand/accueil).

<hr> 

<img src="/img/cyrielle.jpg" width="150"> **Cyrielle Ballester** (engineer 2026) is working on a citizen science program to monitor animal communities along the Lez river in Montpellier.

<hr> 

<img src="/img/lea.jpg" width="130"> **[Léa Pautrel](https://www.terroiko.fr/fr/lea-pautrel)** (post-doc 2026-2027) is investigating the use of deep learning coupled with species distribution models to map animal populations in Occitanie.

<hr> 

<img src="/img/alix.jpg" width="150"> **[Alix Cosquer](https://alixcosquer.com/)** (post-doc 2025-2027) works on a framework for assessing scientific outreach actions. This is joint work with Charlotte Francesiaz from the [French Office of Biodiversity](https://ofb.gouv.fr/) and colleagues from the city and metropolis of Montpellier.  

<hr>

![](/img/simon.jpeg)
**[Simon Lacombe](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Simon-Lacombe-3)** (PhD 2023-2026) is studying the drivers of European otter recolonization in France using non-invasive sampling and statistical ecology. He is co-supervised by Sébastien Devillard. 

<hr> 

<img src="/img/cyril.jpg" width="150"> **[Cyril Milleret](http://cyrilmilleret.info/)** (post-doc 2024-2027) works on spatial explicit capture-recapture models for estimating the abundance of wolf in France. This is joint work with Christophe Duchamp and Jean-Michel Vandel from [French Office of Biodiversity](https://ofb.gouv.fr/).

<hr> 

<img src="/img/cassie.jpg" width="150"> **[Cassie Speakman](https://cassiespeakman.github.io/)** (post-doc 2024-2026) works on the population consequences of human disturbance on small carnivores. This is joint work with Sandrine Ruette from [French Office of Biodiversity](https://ofb.gouv.fr/).

<hr> 

<img src="/img/carolina.jpg" width="150"> **[Carolina Nogueira](https://www.cibio.up.pt/en/people/details/carolina-nogueira/)** (PhD 2025-2028) works on the large-scale assessment of wildlife translocations success across Africa. This is joint work with Pedro Monterroso, Angela Gaylard from [CIBIO](https://www.cibio.up.pt/en/) at the university of Porto.
